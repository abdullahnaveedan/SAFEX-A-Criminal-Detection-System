ALGORITHEM

      In Safex, we employ advanced algorithms such as MTCNN (Multi-Task Cascaded Convolutional Networks) and FaceNet to power our criminal detection system. MTCNN is a state-of-the-art face detection algorithm that accurately locates and extracts facial features from surveillance video frames. This algorithm enables us to precisely identify individuals within the footage. Additionally, we leverage the FaceNet algorithm, which utilizes deep learning techniques to create high-dimensional face embeddings. These embeddings allow us to compare and match faces across different frames and even across different videos, aiding in criminal identification and tracking. By harnessing the power of these cutting-edge algorithms, Safex ensures accurate and efficient criminal detection to enhance security measures.

WORKING PRINCIPLE

      The working principle of Safex, a criminal detection system, revolves around the utilization of MTCNN (Multi-task Cascaded Convolutional Networks) and FaceNet algorithms. MTCNN is employed to detect and localize human faces within CCTV camera feeds, while FaceNet is used for face recognition and comparison. The system works by capturing video streams from CCTV cameras and applying the MTCNN algorithm to identify and extract human faces. These faces are then passed through the FaceNet algorithm, which generates a unique facial embedding or representation for each individual. To identify potential criminals, Safex compares the facial embeddings of detected individuals with a pre-existing database of known criminals. If a match is found, an alert is triggered, notifying security personnel or authorities about the potential threat. Through the combination of MTCNN, FaceNet, and the efficient design of the web application using Django, Safex provides an effective and automated solution for detecting criminals, enhancing security, and aiding in crime prevention.
